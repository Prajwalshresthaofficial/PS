Staying Self-Motivated During CA Exam Prep

Real strategies I use to keep going when motivation dips, exams feel overwhelming, and the finish line seems far away.

Published on July 11, 2026 • By Prajwal Shrestha

The Motivation Rollercoaster

Real talk: CA exam preparation is a rollercoaster. Some days I'm pumped up, finishing chapters and solving practice problems. Other days, I open a book and think "What's the point?" The difference between people who pass and those who don't isn't just intelligence - it's staying motivated when things get hard. Here's what actually keeps me going.

Tip 1: Connect Your "Why"

The biggest mistake I made early: trying to study just to pass the exam. That's not enough fuel. When I hit difficult chapters, that motivation disappears instantly.

What actually worked:

I asked myself: "Why do I want to be a CA?" Not the generic "good job" answer, but the real reason.

  • 🎯 For me: I want to work with startups and help them grow. CA skills are essential for that.
  • When studying auditing now, I think: "This helps me understand company risks - useful for my goal."
  • When taxes feel boring, I remember: "A CA who understands tax strategy can save companies money."

Find YOUR why. Write it down. Read it when motivation dips.

Tip 2: Track Small Wins

When you're studying for 2 years, the end goal feels impossibly far. Small wins are your fuel.

My tracking method:

  • ✓ Completed one full chapter? Write it down.
  • ✓ Got 80% on a practice test? Celebrate it.
  • ✓ Understood a concept that confused you last week? That's progress.
  • ✓ Helped a friend understand something? You're getting stronger.

Bonus: Keep a visible list (on your wall, phone, anywhere). Bad study day? Read your wins list. You're stronger than you think.

Tip 3: Study With Purpose, Not Hours

I used to measure success by hours studied. "I studied 10 hours today, great!" Reality: I was procrastinating, scrolling, and not focused.

What changed everything:

I switched to: "What will I understand today?" Not "how many hours."

  • Before: "I'll study accounting for 3 hours" → Ended up wasting 2 hours
  • Now: "I'll understand depreciation methods by end of day" → Actually achieve it in 1.5 hours
  • Result: More accomplished, less burned out, more motivated the next day

Set outcome goals, not time goals. Quality over quantity = motivation stays high.

Tip 4: The 2-Day Rule

Some days, I don't feel like studying. That's normal. Here's what I do:

The Rule: Never skip more than 1 day

If I miss today, I MUST study tomorrow (even if just 30 minutes). If I miss 2 days in a row, motivation crashes - it gets harder to get back.

The beautiful part: It's just 1 day. Everyone has off days. But 2 days? That's when you start making excuses.

Even 30 minutes is enough to keep the streak alive. Study momentum is everything.

Tip 5: Study With Others Sometimes

I'm an introvert. I prefer studying alone. But completely solo studying? I got lazy and depressed.

What works:

  • Weekly group study (even 1 hour) → accountability + motivation
  • Teach someone else what you learned → reinforces YOUR understanding + feels productive
  • Study buddy check-ins → "What did you study today?" keeps you honest
  • Even just studying in a cafe → seeing other people work is motivating

You don't need to study together the whole time. Just enough to stay connected and accountable.

Tip 6: Celebrate Before Exams

This sounds weird, but: I celebrate small wins BEFORE exams, not after.

Why it matters:

When you only celebrate after exams, you're telling yourself "You haven't done enough yet." Motivation crashes.

I celebrate finishing chapters, getting 75%+ on practice tests, understanding hard concepts. This keeps me fired up during prep.

Example: Finished financial accounting? Take a night off, do something fun. You EARNED it. Come back refreshed.

Tip 7: When Motivation Really Dips

Sometimes these tips aren't enough. Some weeks, I just feel stuck. Here's what I do:

Take a full day off

Not half-hearted studying. Actual day off. Sleep in, hang out with friends, do something fun. One full day.

Change your study environment

Studying at the same desk for weeks = burnout. Move to a cafe, library, park. Fresh environment = fresh motivation.

Switch what you study

Bored with taxation? Study accounting for a day. Mix it up. Variety keeps your brain interested.

Remember why you started

Go back to that "why" you wrote down. Remember what you wanted. This is the journey to get there.

💡 The Real Secret

Motivation isn't something that happens to you. It's something you build. Day after day. Small decisions. Showing up even when you don't feel like it. Celebrating progress. Connecting your studies to your goals.

The CA exam is long. Your motivation will dip. But with these tips, it'll always come back stronger than before.
