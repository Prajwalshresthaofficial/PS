Effective Time Management for CA Exams

Not the boring "wake up at 5 AM" advice. Real strategies that helped me study while having a life.

Published on July 11, 2026 • By Prajwal Shrestha

My Biggest Mistakes

❌ What I tried: Waking at 4 AM to study - lasted 3 days.

❌ What I tried: Studying 12 hours straight - got brain fried.

❌ What I tried: Ignoring everything else - felt burnt out.

The problem? I was copying others' schedules instead of creating one that fit MY lifestyle. I'm not a 4 AM person, and that's okay.

The Schedule That Actually Works For Me

Here's what I settled on (and you should adapt it to YOUR life):

📖 Morning (7 AM - 9 AM): Revision

Quick recap of what I studied yesterday. Coffee + phone on silent. 20 min per subject.

📖 Work/Classes (9 AM - 5 PM): Stay Focused

Don't think about CA during work. I realized I couldn't study effectively while working anyway.

📝 Evening (6 PM - 8 PM): Main Study Session

New concepts, problem-solving, practice questions. Most focused 2 hours of my day.

📝 Night (9 PM - 10 PM): Light Review

Read notes, watch a YouTube video. Nothing heavy - just keeping things fresh.

📖 Weekends: One Deep Study + One Rest

Saturday: 4-5 hour focused study. Sunday: One full chapter or mock test. Then rest genuinely.

Real Talk About Consistency

I don't study 8 hours a day. Some days I study 2 hours, some days 4. Here's what matters:

  • Consistency beats intensity. 2 hours every day > 12 hours once a week
  • Quality over quantity. 1 hour of focused study = 3 hours of distracted scrolling
  • Rest is productive. A rested brain learns better. I stopped feeling guilty about weekends.
  • Life happens. Some weeks I studied 5 hours total. Life is not perfect and that's fine.

Productivity Hacks That Actually Work

📋 Pomodoro but Different

I use 45 min study + 10 min break (not 25+5). Tried the standard and got frustrated.

📱 Phone Placement

Not in another room (weird). Just face down, notifications off, across the table. Mostly works.

📊 Track It

Simple checkbox: Did I study today? After a few weeks, you don't want to break the chain.

🎵 Background Music

Lo-fi beats or brown noise. No lyrics though - they distract me.

What I Learned About Burnout

I hit a wall after 3 months of non-stop studying. Felt tired, unmotivated, and honestly considering giving up.

Then I realized: Taking a full day off didn't slow my progress. It actually helped me come back stronger. Now I'm comfortable with:

  • One day per week where I don't study at all
  • Hanging out with friends without guilt
  • Watching a movie or playing games
  • Days where I study just 1 hour

✅ The Real Formula

Time Management = Sustainable Study + Realistic Expectations + Rest

Not everyone studies 8+ hours. Top CA students study smartly, not just long. Find YOUR rhythm, stick with it consistently, and be kind to yourself on bad days.
