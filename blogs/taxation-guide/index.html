Income Tax Laws: CA Final Exam Focus Areas

From a CA student's desk: The taxation concepts that show up in exams and how I'm tackling them.

Published on July 11, 2026 • By Prajwal Shrestha

Why This Matters

Taxation is a CA final subject, and honestly? It can feel like information overload. So many provisions, amendments, and exceptions. But here's what I've realized: You don't need to memorize everything. You need to understand the structure and key provisions. Let me share what's clicking for me.

The Basic Structure (Start Here)

Income tax has this flow. Understanding this structure helped me organize everything:

Step 1: Calculate Gross Total Income (GTI)

Salary + Income from House Property + Business Income + Capital Gains + Other Sources

↓

Step 2: Deduct Section 80 Provisions

80C (LIC, PPF), 80D (Insurance), 80E (Interest on Education Loan), etc.

Step 3: Get Taxable Income

Apply tax slab, calculate tax, add surcharge & cess

Key Areas I'm Focusing On

1️⃣ Salary Provisions

What I was confused about: Perquisites, leave encashment, gratuity

Concept: Not all salary components are taxable. Some are exempt. Memorizing which is which took time, but doing 10+ problems helped.

2️⃣ House Property

What I was confused about: When to deduct interest, when to claim depreciation

Concept: Self-occupied property has different rules than let-out property. I made a simple table with both scenarios.

3️⃣ Business Income & Deductions

What I was confused about: What expenses are allowed, what are not

Concept: Golden rule: Must be incurred for earning income. Personal expenses? No. Business expenses? Yes. Practice questions clarify this fast.

4️⃣ Capital Gains

What I was confused about: Short-term vs. long-term, indexation benefits

Concept: Holding period changes everything. Less than 2 years = short-term (higher tax). More than 2 years = long-term (lower tax + indexation benefits).

Common Exam Mistakes I'm Avoiding

  • Reading too fast: Tax problems have details. If I miss one number, the whole answer changes.
  • Skipping the basics: I used to jump to complex provisions without understanding section structure.
  • Ignoring amendments: Tax laws change every year. I've started tracking recent amendments.
  • Not practicing: Tax needs practice. Concept alone isn't enough.

How I'm Studying Taxation

  1. Read the concept and provision once (overview)
  2. Watch someone explain it (YouTube CA channels help)
  3. Do 5-10 simple problems from that provision
  4. Do 5-10 exam-level problems (with twists/combinations)
  5. Create a summary note with formula/key points
  6. Revisit every 2 weeks

📚 Resource I'm Using

  • ✓ CA textbook (official source)
  • ✓ Practice manual for problems
  • ✓ YouTube channels for quick explanations
  • ✓ My own notes + flowcharts for quick revision
  • ✓ Previous year exams (5-10 years back)
