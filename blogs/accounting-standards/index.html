Mastering Accounting Standards for CA Final

My journey understanding NAS - what worked, what didn't, and how I stopped getting confused.

Published on July 11, 2026 • By Prajwal Shrestha

The Confusion Begins

When I first opened the NAS module, I felt overwhelmed. There are so many standards - NAS 1, NAS 2, NAS 8... I thought I had to memorize every word. But after struggling for weeks, I realized that wasn't the right approach. Let me share what actually helped me.

Understanding vs. Memorizing

The biggest mistake I made: trying to memorize standards instead of understanding concepts.

Here's what I learned:

  • Learn the Principle First: Before diving into NAS 2, understand WHY inventory needs to be valued at lower of cost or NRV. The principle makes the rules stick.
  • Connect to Real Examples: I started taking local company examples and applying the standards. It suddenly made sense.
  • Practice Problems: Do at least 5-10 problems from each standard. Patterns emerge.
  • Make Flowcharts: Instead of notes, I created decision trees. "Is this expense capitalized or expensed?" → flowchart → answer.

Standards That Confused Me Most

Here are the ones I struggled with and my shortcuts:

NAS 2: Inventories

What confused me: When to capitalize vs. expense, absorption costing confusion.
My trick: Remember "abnormal" waste goes to P&L, "normal" waste goes to inventory cost.

NAS 8: Accounting Policies

What confused me: Changes in accounting policies vs. estimates vs. errors.
My trick: Policy changes = retrospective, Estimates = prospective, Errors = retrospective.

My Study Strategy That Worked

  1. Read the standard once (Overview): Don't worry about understanding everything. Just know what's there.
  2. Watch a YouTube explanation: I found videos that explained with examples - much clearer than text.
  3. Read again with focus: Now read specific sections you didn't understand.
  4. Do practice problems: Start easy, then do harder exam-level questions.
  5. Teach someone: I explained to my study friend. If they understood, I understood well enough.
  6. Revisit every 2 weeks: Quick revision keeps it fresh.

📏 Key Takeaways for You

  • ✓ Understand the principle, not just the rule
  • ✓ Use flowcharts instead of linear notes
  • ✓ Connect to real-world examples
  • ✓ Practice the same standard 10+ times
  • ✓ Don't try to memorize - let understanding stick naturally
